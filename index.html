Troubleshooting Tips for Common CrashPlan Login Issues

Even with a polished system, you might occasionally encounter a login problem. Here are solutions to the most common CrashPlan login issues, drawn from official support resources [web:8].

"I Can't Sign In" or "Invalid Credentials"

This is often due to a forgotten password or a simple typo. The first step is to carefully re-enter your username and password, checking for typos and ensuring the Caps Lock key is off. If that fails, use the 'Forgot Password?' link on the login page [web:1]. This will guide you through a secure process to reset your password. You may also need to reset your two-factor authentication if it's causing issues [web:3].

The CrashPlan App Fails to Open a Web Browser

If the app attempts to log you in but a browser window doesn't appear, you have two options. First, return to the app and click the 'Retry' button. If it still fails, the app provides a 'Copy' button. Click this to copy the unique sign-in URL, then manually paste it into your web browser to complete the login process. Once you sign in through the browser, the app will automatically be logged in as well [web:3].

"Logged out by authority" Message

This message can sometimes appear in the app. In most cases, you can resolve this by simply clicking 'Sign in with a different account' and trying the login process again. If the problem persists, it could mean your user account or device has been deactivated or blocked by an administrator. In this case, you would need to contact your organization's IT admin or CrashPlan support for assistance [web:3].

"Unable to sign in. Check the server address." Error

This specific error indicates that the username you entered exists on more than one of CrashPlan's servers. To resolve this, you must sign in using the direct URL for your specific server environment (e.g., console.us2.crashplan.com). This ensures you are attempting to authenticate against the correct server for your account [web:3].
